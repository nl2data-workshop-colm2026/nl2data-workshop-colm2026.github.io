NL2Data: Bridging Language Models and Multimodal Data

October 9, 2026 · Hilton Union Square · San Francisco, USA

About the Workshop

Large language models are enabling a new paradigm that goes beyond NL2SQL into full NL2Data workflows: understanding intent, discovering relevant data, orchestrating tools, and producing grounded insights across heterogeneous sources.

NL2Data 2026 focuses on the full lifecycle: semantic representation, data discovery, trustworthy analytics, and evaluation. The workshop brings together NLP, AI, and data systems researchers and practitioners to build reliable, scalable, and democratized data access systems.

Call for Papers

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions on topics including, but not limited to:

  • Semantic Modeling for Multimodal Data

    Unified semantic layers, automated semantic-store construction and enrichment, schema evolution and metric-drift handling, entity resolution and ontology alignment, metadata lineage/provenance, and interoperable standards across text, tables, graphs, and images.

  • Data Discovery through NL2Data

    Natural-language query generation across modalities, disambiguation for underspecified requests, hybrid semantic retrieval, context-aware planning over federated sources, conversational exploration, and multi-hop discovery over structured and unstructured data.

  • LLM-driven Data Intelligence

    Agentic tool orchestration, grounded and verifiable insight generation, human-in-the-loop validation, uncertainty calibration, auditable reasoning traces, and robust analytics pipelines resilient to hallucinations, tool errors, and missing data.

  • Datasets, Benchmarks, and Evaluation

    Real-world NL2Data datasets across domains and modalities, end-to-end task suites, metrics for faithfulness/correctness/reliability/repeatability, long-horizon agent benchmark design, and reproducible evaluation protocols with shared baselines.

Submission Details

We invite submissions up to 9 pages following CoLM formatting guidelines. Reviews will be double-blind via OpenReview. Workshop publications are non-archival; accepted papers will be featured on the workshop website and presented as posters.
